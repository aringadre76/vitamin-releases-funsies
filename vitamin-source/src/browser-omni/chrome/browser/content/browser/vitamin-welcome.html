Vitamin
Privacy-first browsing with built-in data poisoning

What is Vitamin?

Vitamin is a privacy-focused browser built on LibreWolf. It includes all the privacy protections you'd expect, plus something unique: Data Poisoning.

💊

Data Poisoning

Trackers build profiles on you based on your browsing. Vitamin fights back by generating fake browsing activity in the background — polluting your tracker profile with garbage data, making it useless for targeting you.

How It Works

When you activate the poison feature, Vitamin opens background tabs that simulate realistic human browsing — searches, clicks, scrolling — all with randomized fingerprints that look like different browsers and devices.

Trackers see this activity and add it to your profile. But since it's fake and random, your real interests get buried in noise. Ad targeting becomes useless.

Using the Poison Button

Look for the pill icon in your toolbar.

  1. Click once to toggle poison on/off. When active, the icon glows orange.
  2. Right-click to open settings — adjust frequency, max tabs, search engines, and more.
  3. Hover over the button to see live stats: tabs opened, searches performed, sites visited.
⚙️

Settings

Right-click the pill button to customize: how often new tabs open, how many run at once, which search engines to use (Bing and Yahoo are recommended — Google may trigger CAPTCHAs), and whether to auto-start on browser launch.

Tips

• Let it run in the background — the longer it runs, the more noise in your profile
• Use alongside your normal browsing — your real activity mixes with fake data
• Don't worry about the tabs — they open in isolated containers and close automatically
